# Hurricane Risk Notes (Sample)

- Storm intensity is often described by maximum sustained wind speed and minimum central pressure.
- Rapid intensification refers to a large increase in wind speed over a short period (definition may vary by agency).
- Coastal risk depends on storm surge, rainfall, wind, and population vulnerability.
- Uncertainty grows with forecast horizon; communicate confidence and assumptions.

## Practical guidance
- Compare storms by time series of intensity and track, not just peak values.
- Combine hazard + exposure + vulnerability for a risk score narrative.
